Welcome to
ANTARIKSH

Welcome to the Antariksh Astronomy Club, an inter-institutional club dedicated to fostering a passion for Astronomy and Astrophysics. Founded in 2009 under the umbrella of Vishwakarma Institutes, including Vishwakarma Institute of Technology, Vishwakarma Institute of Information Technology, and Vishwakarma University, the club has a rich legacy of nurturing countless successful careers in research. Our primary agenda is research and the sharing of knowledge, providing a vibrant and inclusive community for students and enthusiasts alike.

About Us

The Antariksh Astronomy Club, also known as Antariksh Club, was established by three visionaries: Raunak, Abhishek, and alumnus Jameer sir. Over the years, our members have contributed to esteemed organizations such as ISRO, IUCAA, IISERs, and prestigious institutions abroad. We aim to create a platform for like-minded individuals to share knowledge, engage in hands-on experiences, and promote personal development.

Our activities include hosting talks, workshops, and events with distinguished professionals, facilitating practical learning through star-gazing sessions and observatory tours, and encouraging participation in research projects and technical initiatives. We also emphasize soft skills development, organizational skills, and outreach activities to engage with the broader community and share the wonders of the universe

By joining Antariksh Club, you become part of a thriving community dedicated to exploring the cosmos and advancing space science. Whether you're a seasoned researcher or a passionate amateur, there's a place for you in our club.

GMRT Visit, Khodad

The Giant Metrewave Radio Telescope, located near Narayangaon, Pune in India, is an array of thirty fully steerable parabolic radio telescopes of 45 metre diameter, observing at metre wavelengths. It is the largest and most sensitive radio telescope array in the world at low frequencies.

Internal Talks and Discussions

Evolution of Astronomy

The Antariksh - Astronomy Club at Vishwakarma Institute Of Technology held its second internal talk, "Evolution of Astronomy," on August 11, 2023. Sahil Sawant, a dedicated club member, discussed space-time paradoxes, ancient Indian astronomers, Galileo's observations, Sir Isaac Newton's contributions, Albert Einstein's theories, modern cosmic quests, and the impact of radio astronomy and the IUCAA. The event was attended by 15 volunteers and highlighted the importance of astronomy in our understanding.

Life Cycle of Stars

The Antariksh - Astronomy Club hosted an internal talk on the "Life Cycle of Stars" on September 8, 2023. Anas Ansari, a core member of the club, explained the intricate stages of star formation, including the formation of Carina Nebulae, proto-stage stars, medium-sized stars, red giants, super red giants, planetary nebulae, white dwarfs, supernova explosions, hypernovae, and neutron stars or black holes. The talk provided a deeper understanding of the life cycle of stars and their evolution..

Death of Stars and Intro to Black Holes

The Antariksh - Astronomy Club hosted an engaging internal talk on September 16, 2023, featuring Harsh Jalnekar, a core member of the club. The session covered topics such as the "Death of a Star" and an "Introduction to Black Holes." Key highlights included the cosmic drama of supernovas, hypernovas, gamma-ray bursts, kilonovas, and neutron stars. Jalnekar also introduced black holes and their impact on the cosmos, addressing questions related to star naming, magnetars, and the Big Bang.
